Giving Wings to
India's Underprivileged

NayePankh Foundation is one of India's largest student-led NGOs — feeding the hungry, educating the forgotten, and uplifting over 2 lakh lives across Uttar Pradesh.

Born during the darkest days of COVID-19, NayePankh started as a small student effort to feed people who had nothing. The name means "new wings" — a belief that every person, no matter how broken, deserves a chance to fly again.


Today we operate in Kanpur, Ghaziabad, and beyond — distributing food to families and stray animals, providing sanitary products, clothes, and running education drives for communities left behind by society.

What We Do

Programs that make a difference

Six pillars of change — each addressing a real, urgent need faced by India's most vulnerable communities.

🍱
Food Distribution

We distribute freshly prepared meals to underprivileged families, migrant workers, the elderly, and stray animals — because hunger does not discriminate.

📚
Education Drives

From street-side tutoring to stationery donations, we work to keep children in school and give them the tools they need to build a future.

🌸
Menstrual Hygiene

We distribute sanitary napkins and run awareness campaigns to break the silence around menstrual health among young women and girls.

👗
Clothing Aid

Seasonal clothing drives ensure that poor families face winter with dignity, not desperation — one blanket and one warm shirt at a time.

💊
Health Camps

Free health check-ups, basic medicines, and hygiene kits reach communities where government healthcare remains a distant promise.

🌱
Community Empowerment

Skill-building workshops and awareness sessions help people find sustainable pathways out of poverty — beyond short-term relief.
